Alternatives to Clip in Human Hair Extensions

There are many alternatives to clip in human hair extensions, including tape-in hair extensions, halo hair extensions, and more. Each type of extension has its own set of pros and cons, so it's important to do your research before deciding which method is right for you.

Tape-in hair extensions are a popular alternative to clip-ins. They are attached to your natural hair using double-sided tape, and can last up to eight weeks. Tape-ins are less damaging than other types of extensions, but they can be tricky to remove if you're not careful.

Halo hair extensions are another option. They are attached to your head with a comfortable headband, and can be worn for up to twelve hours at a time. Halo extensions are easy to put in and take out, but they can be costly.

If you're looking for a more permanent solution, there are also options like fusion and Microlink hair extensions. These methods attach the extensions directly to your natural hair, and can last for several months. However, they can be expensive and time-consuming to install.

Steps to Take Care of Your Hair Extensions

If you're considering clip in human hair extensions, congratulations! You're about to make your hair look amazing. But before you get too excited, there are a few things you need to know about taking care of your new hair extensions. Here are the steps you need to take to ensure your extensions stay healthy and looking great:

  • Brush them daily: Just like your own hair, your hair extensions need to be brushed daily. This will help prevent tangles and keep them looking smooth and shiny.
  • Wash them regularly: You should wash your hair extensions at least once a week, using a mild shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using any products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out the hair and cause damage.
  • Use a heat protectant: If you plan on using any heated styling tools on your hair extensions, make sure to use a heat protectant spray or cream first. This will help prevent damage from the heat and keep your extensions looking healthy.
  • Avoid sleeping with them: It's best to avoid sleeping with your clip in human hair extensions in, as this can cause damage or matting over time. If you must sleep with them in, be sure to use a satin pillowcase to help minimize friction.
  • Store them properly: When you're not wearing your clip in hair extensions, be sure to store them properly in a cool, dry place. This will help prolong their lifespan and keep them looking good in long run.
