Types of Hair Extensions

Hand tied extensions are becoming an increasingly popular option for women seeking to add length and volume to their natural hair. There are many different types of hand tied hair extensions on the market, each with its own unique benefits. Here is a breakdown of the most popular types of hand tied hair extensions to help you choose the best option for your needs:

  • Clip-in Hair Extensions: Clip-in hair extensions are temporary hair extensions that can be easily clipped into your natural hair to add length and volume. They are a great option for those who want to try out hair extensions without making a long-term commitment.
  • Tape-in Hair Extensions: Tape-in hair extensions are semi-permanent hair extensions that are adhered to your natural hair with tape. They last longer than clip-in extensions but can be damaging to your natural hair if not installed correctly.
  • Micro bead Hair Extensions: Micro bead hair extensions are semi-permanent hair extensions that are attached to your natural hair with small beads. They are less damaging than tape-in extensions but can be more difficult to remove.
  • Fusion Hair Extensions: Fusion hair extensions are permanent hair extension method where strands of extension hair are bonded directly to your natural hair with heat or adhesive. This method is very damaging to your natural hair and should only be performed by a professional stylist.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Hand Tied Hair Extensions

When it comes to hand tied extensions, there are many different types and methods available. One of the newer methods is hand tied hair extensions. This method has both advantages and disadvantages that you should be aware of before making a decision about whether or not to get them. 

The main advantage of hand tied hair extensions is that they are very natural looking. The process involves tying individual strands of hair to your own natural hair with a small silicone bead. This results in a seamless blend that looks completely natural. Another advantage of this method is that it is much less damaging to your natural hair than other methods such as fusion or tape-in extensions. Since the individual strands are not glued or taped down, there is less risk of damage to your own hair.

The main disadvantage of hand tied extensions is that they are more expensive than other methods. They also require more time and patience to install, since each strand must be individually tied. Additionally, they need to be professionally installed and maintained, which can add to the overall cost.

How to Look After Hair Extensions?

If you're considering getting hand tied hair extensions, or have already made the plunge, congratulations! Hand tied hair extensions are a fantastic way to add length, volume, and/or color to your natural hair. But as with anything new, there is a bit of a learning curve when it comes to properly caring for your new locks. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about how to look after your hand tied hair extensions, from washing and brushing to styling and sleeping.

  • Washing & Brushing

To avoid tangles and matting, it's important to brush your hand tied extensions before washing them. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using a soft bristled brush or comb. When you're ready to wash them, use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed specifically for extensions. Avoid using hot water, as this can damage the hair. Gently massage the shampoo into the scalp and rinse thoroughly. Follow with conditioner, again being careful not to tug or pull at the hair. Rinse well and then blot dry with a towel.

  • Styling

Hand tied extensions can be styled just like your natural hair! Use a heat protectant spray before blow drying or using any heated styling tools. When curling or flat ironing, be sure not to wrap the hair too tightly around the barrel - this can cause breakage. Let your hair cool completely before brushing out any curls.

What type of hair is best for hand tied extensions?

If you're considering hand tied extensions, then you're probably wondering what type of hair is best for the application. The good news is that almost any type of hair can be used for hand tied extensions, although there are some types that work better than others.

The best type of hair to use for hand tied extensions is virgin hair. This is because virgin hair has not been chemically treated in any way, so it's stronger and more durable than other types of hair. It also has a natural texture that blends well with most people's natural hair, making it less noticeable that you're wearing extensions.

If you can't find virgin hair, the next best option is Remy hair. Remy hair is also high-quality and durable, but it may be slightly more expensive than virgin hair. However, it's still a good option if you want your hand tied hair extensions to look natural and last long.

Finally, if you're on a budget or you just want to try out hand tied extensions before commit to them long-term, then synthetic hair is a good option. Synthetic hair looks and feels very realistic, although it's not as strong or durable as real human hair. However, it's a great option if you want to experiment with hand tied hair extensions without spending too much money.

How long do hand tied extensions last?

Hand tied hair extensions can last anywhere from 3 to 6 months, depending on how well you take care of them and how often you wear them. To extend the life of your hand tied extensions, be sure to:

  • Wash them with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Brush them gently with a soft bristled brush
  • Avoid sleeping with them in
  • Limit heat styling
  • Avoid getting them wet (i.e. swimming, rain) as much as possible

How to Style Hand Tied Hair Extensions?

Hand tied hair extensions are a type of extension that is sewn onto a weft and then hand-tied to your natural hair. This method is one of the most popular methods for extensions, as it results in a very natural look. The main thing to keep in mind when styling hand tied hair extensions is that you need to be extra careful not to damage the wefts. Here are some tips on how to style your hand tied hair extensions:

  • Start by brushing through your hand tied extensions with a soft bristle brush. This will help to remove any tangles and knots.
  • Use a wide tooth comb to gently detangle your hair, starting from the bottom and working your way up.
  • When you shampoo your hair, be sure to use a sulphate free shampoo and conditioner. This will help to protect the wefts and keep them looking their best.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hand tied hair extensions, as this can damage the wefts. If you must use heat, be sure to use a heat protectant spray beforehand.
  • To style your hand tied hair extensions, simply air dry or diffuse them until they are completely dry. You can then style them as you would normally style your own hair.
